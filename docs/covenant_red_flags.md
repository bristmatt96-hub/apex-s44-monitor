# Covenant Red Flags for European HY

## Overview

ZIRP-era (2019-2022) high yield documentation often featured "covenant-lite" structures that favor borrowers. This guide identifies red flags that increase LME risk.

---

## Documentation Scoring Framework

### Risk Levels

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1-2 | LOW | Strong creditor protections, post-2023 docs with blockers |
| 3-4 | MEDIUM | Standard HY docs, some protections |
| 5-6 | HIGH | Covenant-lite, ZIRP-era, limited protections |
| 7-8 | CRITICAL | Aggressive docs, multiple loopholes, active LME risk |

---

## Red Flag Categories

### 1. Unrestricted Subsidiary Provisions

**HIGH RISK:**
- Unlimited ability to designate unrestricted subsidiaries
- No cap on value transferable to unrestricted subs
- IP/brand can be transferred to unrestricted subs
- No J.Crew blocker language

**MEDIUM RISK:**
- Designation permitted but capped (e.g., 10% of assets)
- Some limitations on IP transfers
- Builder basket governs designation capacity

**LOW RISK:**
- J.Crew blocker present
- Material IP explicitly excluded from transfers
- Strict designation criteria
- Unrestricted subs cannot hold material assets

---

### 2. Permitted Investments Basket

**HIGH RISK:**
- Unlimited investments in unrestricted subs
- "Grower" basket tied to EBITDA (can expand significantly)
- No cap on intercompany loans
- Investment capacity exceeds 50% of EBITDA

**MEDIUM RISK:**
- Capped basket with reasonable limits
- Some restrictions on unrestricted sub investments
- Grower basket with cap

**LOW RISK:**
- Tight fixed-dollar cap
- Explicit restrictions on investments in unrestricted subs
- No grower component

---

### 3. Restricted Payments Capacity

**HIGH RISK:**
- Large builder basket (accumulated CNI)
- Grower basket tied to EBITDA
- Unlimited dividends to parent
- Capacity exceeds 1x EBITDA
- Recent dividend recaps despite leverage

**MEDIUM RISK:**
- Capped RP basket
- Builder basket with reasonable limits
- Some dividend restrictions

**LOW RISK:**
- Tight RP covenant
- No builder basket or strictly capped
- Dividend blocker at leverage threshold
- Cash sweep requirements

---

### 4. Asset Sale Covenant

**HIGH RISK:**
- 100% reinvestment rights (no prepayment required)
- Long reinvestment period (18+ months)
- Broad definition of "reinvestment"
- No FMV requirement
- Intercompany sales permitted at non-market terms

**MEDIUM RISK:**
- Partial prepayment requirement
- 12-month reinvestment period
- Some FMV requirements

**LOW RISK:**
- Mandatory prepayment of asset sale proceeds
- Short reinvestment period
- Strict FMV requirements
- Third-party sales only

---

### 5. Debt Incurrence

**HIGH RISK:**
- Low ratio test (e.g., 2.0x Fixed Charge Coverage)
- Large permitted debt baskets
- Ratio debt can be secured pari passu
- No cap on incremental facilities
- "Freebie" baskets not subject to ratio

**MEDIUM RISK:**
- Moderate ratio test
- Capped permitted debt baskets
- Some restrictions on secured debt

**LOW RISK:**
- Tight ratio test (e.g., 4.0x minimum coverage)
- Small permitted debt baskets
- Incremental debt limited and junior
- Anti-layering provisions

---

### 6. Lien Covenant / Collateral

**HIGH RISK:**
- Large permitted liens baskets
- Ability to prime existing debt
- No "equal and ratable" provisions
- 1L/1O vs 1L/2O ambiguity
- Collateral release without consent

**MEDIUM RISK:**
- Capped permitted liens
- Some priming restrictions
- Equal and ratable for specified debt

**LOW RISK:**
- Tight permitted liens
- Anti-priming provisions
- Clear intercreditor terms
- Collateral release requires consent

---

### 7. Amendment / Waiver Thresholds

**HIGH RISK:**
- Simple majority (50%+1) can amend most provisions
- Lien subordination not a "sacred right"
- No requirement for pro rata treatment
- Open market purchase loophole

**MEDIUM RISK:**
- Supermajority (66.7%) for material amendments
- Some sacred rights protected
- Limited open market purchase rights

**LOW RISK:**
- 100% consent for lien subordination
- Explicit anti-Serta provisions
- Pro rata sharing mandatory
- No open market purchase loophole

---

### 8. Guarantee / Structural Subordination

**HIGH RISK:**
- Guarantor release permitted easily
- Intercompany loans to non-guarantors permitted
- Complex holdco structure
- Value at non-guarantor subs

**MEDIUM RISK:**
- Some guarantor release restrictions
- Limited intercompany loan capacity
- Most value at guarantor level

**LOW RISK:**
- Tight guarantor coverage (95%+ of EBITDA)
- Strict guarantor release conditions
- No material non-guarantor subs

## ZIRP-Era Documentation Vintage Guide

| Vintage | Typical Quality | Notes |
|---------|----------------|-------|
| Pre-2015 | MEDIUM | Before worst excesses |
| 2015-2018 | MEDIUM-HIGH | Covenant erosion beginning |
| 2019-2021 | HIGH RISK | Peak ZIRP, covenant-lite epidemic |
| 2022 | HIGH RISK | Late cycle, aggressive docs |

## Quick Reference: Specific Red Flag Terms

### Terms That Should Concern You

| Term | Risk | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| "Unrestricted Subsidiary" | HIGH | Asset stripping vehicle |
| "Permitted Investment" | MEDIUM-HIGH | Leakage pathway |
| "Builder Basket" | MEDIUM | Accumulated capacity for RP/investment |
| "Grower Basket" | HIGH | Expands with EBITDA, no fixed cap |
| "Available Amount" | MEDIUM | Accumulated CNI for RP |
| "Ratio Debt" | MEDIUM | Incurrence not capped if ratio met |
| "Open Market Purchase" | HIGH | Selective buyback loophole |
| "Permitted Liens" | MEDIUM | Priming pathway |
| "Portability" | MEDIUM | Survives change of control |

### Terms You Want to See

| Term | Protection | Why |
|------|-----------|-----|
| "J.Crew Blocker" | HIGH | Prevents IP drop-down |
| "Anti-Serta" | HIGH | Prevents up-tier exchange |
| "Sacred Rights" | HIGH | Requires 100% consent |
| "Pro Rata Sharing" | MEDIUM | Prevents selective treatment |
| "Anti-Layering" | MEDIUM | Limits debt subordination |
| "Maintenance Covenant" | HIGH | Tested quarterly, not just at incurrence |
| "Equal and Ratable" | MEDIUM | Lien parity protection |

## Monitoring Headlines

When you see these headlines, assess documentation immediately:

| Headline Pattern | Urgency | Action |
|-----------------|---------|--------|
| "[Company] hires Houlihan/Evercore/PJT" | CRITICAL | Check LME provisions |
| "[Company] exploring strategic alternatives" | HIGH | Assess all docs |
| "Ad hoc group forms" | HIGH | Identify who's in/out |
| "[Company] designates unrestricted sub" | CRITICAL | J.Crew in progress |
| "[Company] announces exchange offer" | CRITICAL | Assess participation |
| "[Sponsor] takes dividend" | HIGH | Check RP capacity |
| "Intercompany reorganization" | HIGH | Potential drop-down |
| "[Company] sells [asset] to affiliate" | CRITICAL | Value leakage |

## European vs US Documentation

| Feature | US Typical | European Typical |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| Governing Law | NY | English/Lux |
| Restructuring Forum | Ch.11 / State Court | Scheme/WHOA/Local |
| LME Precedents | Extensive | Growing |
| Creditor Protections | Varies | Often weaker |
| Enforcement | Established | Jurisdiction-dependent |

European HY often has:
- Less developed case law on LME tactics
- More complex multi-jurisdiction structures
- Greater structural subordination risk
- Less liquid secondary markets (harder to exit)
